A mode in a program is a setting that determines how input is handled. Examples are:

insert vs. overtype mode in a word processor: characters are inserted or they overtype existing text
drag-and-drop: during the drag operation, the mouse doesn't carry out its normal functions
constrained resizing: when holding down the shift key while resizing an image, many graphics programs constrain the mouse move to preserve the aspect ratio of the image
modal dialog: when a dialog pops up, the user must supply information and dismiss it; all other operations are temporarily suspended
