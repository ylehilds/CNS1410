While integers cannot represent numbers as large or as small as can be done with floating point numbers, the difference between successive integers is a single step, that is 1. There are no values in between, so a test for quality will either succeed or fail. By comparison, floating-point numbers are only accurate to within about 14 digits, after which point they are rounded. For example, a test of two identical numbers, e.g. sqrt(r * r) == r can fail. The best that can be said is that two floating-point numbers are equivalent within a certain tolerance.

To test if an integer equals ten use:

   int n;
   . . .
   if (n == 10) . . .

To test if a floating point number equals ten use:

   float x;
   . . .
   if(fabs(x - 10) / max(fabs(x), 10) <= EPSILON ) . . .
